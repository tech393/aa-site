# PHOTO STYLE GUIDE — Awakened Academy®

*The yay/nay filter for every image on the AA site, in emails, in ads, in the brochure, anywhere with the AA logo on it. If a picture doesn't pass this filter, it doesn't go up.*

*Built from: Michael's curated Shutterstock archive (266 images, May 2026) + the AA brochure photo set + brand-design.md.*

*Owner: Michael chooses, Dan executes. Updated when the canon shifts.*

---

## THE ONE-LINE RULE

**Warm golden-hour light. Vast contemplative space. The viewer projects herself INTO the picture — she is never being looked at by it.**

Every image is asking her one question: *can you feel yourself there?*

If yes → it's in.
If she feels she's being marketed to → it's out.

## THE 5-QUESTION YAY/NAY FILTER

Open any candidate image. Run the five questions in order. Stop at the first NO.

| # | Question | What "yes" looks like | What "no" looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Light** | Golden hour, sunrise, sunset, soft warm side-light, stars over a glowing horizon | Midday sun, fluorescent, cold daylight, harsh shadow, flat overhead studio lighting |
| 2 | **Palette** | Warm gold, orange, pink, peach, deep purple, dusty rose, earth tones, navy-to-pink gradient sky | Cool blue-white, stark grey, corporate teal-on-white, neon, oversaturated, anything sterile |
| 3 | **Subject** | Vast nature (sky, mountain, ocean, desert, forest at dusk) — OR a person who is NOT facing the camera (back-shot, silhouette, hand detail, footprints) | Person facing camera, smiling at lens, posed, posing with a laptop, group shot, meeting room, anything that looks like a stock-photo headshot |
| 4 | **Framing & space** | Wide negative space. The subject is small in a big world. Room to breathe. She can imagine standing there | Tight, busy, cluttered, edge-to-edge subject, no room for the viewer to enter |
| 5 | **Vibe** | Quiet, contemplative, sacred, inviting. Feels like the inside of meditation | Loud, kinetic, sales-y, promotional, "look at me" |

**Scoring: All five YES → YAY. One NO → second opinion. Two or more NO → NAY.**

## SHOT 10 RULES — THE REAL PEOPLE CONNECTION CATEGORY

This is the hardest shot category to do right and the easiest to ruin. The whole guide can stay clean and then ONE bad headshot collapses the brand into "another coaching website."

**Strict rules. ALL must pass:**

1. **Looking AT something meaningful, not AT the lens.** Out a window. Into nature. At another human. At a book. At their own hands. NEVER directly into camera with a "trust me" smile.
2. **Natural light.** Window, outdoor, golden hour. No flash, no studio softbox, no ring light.
3. **Candid feel, not headshot perfection.** Slightly imperfect framing reads more real than perfectly centered. Mid-laugh, mid-thought, mid-action.
4. **Warm clothing, simple.** Earth tones, linen, white, gold, cream. NO blazers, NO bright corporate colors, NO branded shirts.
5. **Real backgrounds.** A real room, a real kitchen, a real garden, a real beach. NEVER white seamless studio, NEVER fake "office" background.
6. **One person at a time** for hero use. Two people MAX for testimonial/community shots, and they must be interacting with each other, not posing toward camera.
7. **Age-appropriate to Annie.** 30–60. Not 20-something. Not corporate-50.

**Auto-NAY for Shot 10 specifically:**
- Headshot against white background → NAY
- Crossed arms, "boss pose" → NAY
- Laughing with a salad → NAY
- "Diverse smiling team" stock → NAY
- Holding a clipboard or wearing a headset → NAY
- Anyone posing in front of an obviously fake "spiritual" background → NAY

**For the AA brand specifically, Shot 10 candidates come from:**
- **The existing 216 AA people-photos library** — first-priority source for graduate, testimonial, and community shots. These are REAL people who got real results. Stock can't compete with that.
- **A new Michael + Arielle photo shoot** — recommended Q3 budget item. 2–4 hours with a real photographer in good outdoor settings produces 30–50 usable shots covering all future needs.
- **AI generation** — for atmospheric/distance shots only, per Section below. NOT for face-forward founder shots.
- **Shutterstock (last resort)** — only when the message is generic enough that no real person matters. Search terms: `woman golden hour candid`, `woman 40 meditation outdoor warm`, `woman reading book window light`, `mature woman natural light contemplative`.

## AI IMAGE GENERATION — THREE PATHS

You asked: can we use AI to upgrade existing Michael photos and put him in different situations that look real? Yes — three paths with very different risk/reward profiles. Pick by use case.

### Path A — UPSCALE existing photos (DO THIS WEEK)

What it is: take old, low-res, or compressed photos of Michael, Arielle, and graduates → run through an AI upscaler → get sharp, high-resolution versions that look professionally shot.

**Tools (best 2026 options):**
- **Topaz Photo AI** (~$199 one-time) — best for hero shots
- **Photoshop Generative Fill** (Adobe Creative Cloud) — best if Dan already has Adobe
- **Magnific AI** (~$10/mo) — fastest, web-based, very good

**Risk:** near zero. The photo is real; we're just sharpening it.
**Return:** high. Probably solves 60–70% of the "I need more Michael photos" problem just by making existing ones presentable.
**Operator:** Dan. ~2–4 hours to process the whole `ALL-PEOPLE-IMAGES-2026-05-19/` Michael + Arielle subset.
**Recommended:** YES. Start this week.

### Path B — TRAIN A LORA (Michael's likeness, generate new scenes)

What it is: take 20–30 photos of Michael (different angles, lighting, expressions) → train a custom AI model that "knows" his face → generate new images of him in any setting via text prompts ("Michael walking on a beach at sunset, golden hour, shot from behind").

**Tools (best 2026 options):**
- **Replicate.com** — train Flux LoRA, ~$50 per training run, then ~$0.05/image
- **Astria.ai** — similar, faster setup
- **ComfyUI + local Flux** — if Dan wants to run it locally, more setup but free per image

**Risk:** medium. The honest constraints:
- Hands and small details can still go wrong
- Skin texture sometimes reads "AI" to a trained eye
- Clothing, beard length, accessories may drift between images
- If the audience clocks it as AI, the brand integrity argument ("AA is the real thing vs. scammers") takes a hit

**Use AI-generated Michael ONLY for:**
- Shot 5 (from behind / silhouette / distant) — face not the focus
- Shot 7 (empty contemplative space with implied presence)
- Atmospheric blog inline shots where Michael appears small in a landscape
- Background imagery, not portraits

**Do NOT use AI-generated Michael for:**
- Founder photos
- About page
- Brochure portraits
- Course author bios
- Anywhere Annie is supposed to feel "this is the real human I'm trusting"

**Operator:** Dan + a contractor familiar with Flux LoRAs. ~$200–500 total for setup + first batch.
**Recommended:** YES, but scoped — only for distant/atmospheric shots, not portraits.

### Path C — IMAGE-TO-IMAGE with reference (variations on existing photos)

What it is: take one good photo of Michael → use AI to generate variations (same person, different lighting, different background, different angle).

**Tools (best 2026 options):**
- **Flux Kontext** — best for "same person, edit the scene"
- **Midjourney v7** with `--cref` (character reference)
- **Sora image mode**

**Risk:** lower than LoRA training (no model to train) but variability is higher (each generation can drift).
**Return:** medium. Good for quick experiments. Less consistent than LoRA at scale.
**Operator:** Dan, anyone comfortable with prompt-based tools.
**Recommended:** Use opportunistically. Not the primary system.

### Path D (added for completeness) — REAL PHOTO SHOOT

What it is: book a photographer for 2–4 hours, shoot Michael and Arielle in 3–5 outdoor settings that match the canon (beach at golden hour, forest, mountain overlook, garden, indoor warm-light).

**Risk:** zero (photos are real).
**Return:** highest — produces 30–50 hero-quality photos covering all canon shot types for both founders.
**Cost:** $500–$2,000 depending on photographer.
**Operator:** Michael + Dan.
**Recommended:** YES for Q3 budget. Solves the founder-portrait gap that AI can't safely fill.

## THE WORKFLOW — HOW DAN USES THIS GUIDE

*Three-tier funnel. Dan owns Tier 1 and Tier 2. Michael owns Tier 3 only.*

### Tier 1 — Dan Eliminates Obvious NAYs (5–15 min per batch)

Open this guide. Read the **AUTO-NAY LIST** section. Open the candidate images in batches (Shutterstock search results, Michael's archive, anywhere). For each image, ask: does it hit any auto-NAY?

- Eye contact / posed at camera → NAY
- Office / desk / meeting room → NAY
- Cold lighting → NAY
- "Diverse smiling team" stock → NAY
- White-background headshot → NAY
- Anyone in business attire / blazer / headset → NAY

Drag NAYs to a "rejected" folder. Don't agonize — auto-NAYs aren't borderline.

**Expected pass rate:** 50–70% of any random Shutterstock dump gets cut here. Takes minutes, not hours.

### Tier 2 — AI Scores the Survivors (5–10 min per batch)

Open a Claude (or any LLM) chat. Attach this `PHOTO-STYLE-GUIDE.md` file. Drop in the Tier 1 survivors (batches of ~20–30).

**The prompt to use:**
> "Score each of these images against PHOTO-STYLE-GUIDE.md. For each, give me: filename → YAY / Borderline / NAY → one-line reason. Group your output by verdict."

Claude/AI returns a structured list. Dan keeps the YAYs and Borderlines, kills the rest.

**Expected output at Tier 2:** ~5–10 strong YAYs per 30 candidates. That's the shortlist.

### Tier 3 — Michael Approves (2–5 min)

Dan presents Michael with the shortlist per slot — 5–10 images max. Michael picks one (or none, and Dan goes back to Tier 1).

**That's it. End-to-end: ~30 min per image slot. For 6 blog placeholders: ~3 hours of Dan, 30 min of Michael.**

### When to pull more candidates (just-in-time, not just-in-case)

Don't curate "the perfect 500-image library for the future." That's busywork. Pull images for the specific slots that need filling **right now**. The guide enforces consistency between sessions, so the v1 blog set will visually match the v10 blog set even if they're months apart.

**Image slots in the current queue:**
1. Four Words article — 3 placeholder images (hero + 2 inline)
2. Two Mindsets article — 3 placeholder images (hero + 2 inline)
3. Future blog articles — 3 per article going forward
4. Opt-in page heroes — 1 per page (4 pages live, more coming)
5. Sales pages — 5–8 per page
6. Ads — varies by campaign

Calculate demand. Pull only what's needed. Move on.

### Dan's deliverable to Michael per slot

A single message:
> "Slot: [page name] / [position]. Canon shot category: [#X — Name]. Candidates: [5 image filenames or links]. AI scored these as YAY. My pick: [filename]. Your call?"

Michael replies one word: **YES** or **NEXT**. If NEXT, Dan brings the second-best from the shortlist.
